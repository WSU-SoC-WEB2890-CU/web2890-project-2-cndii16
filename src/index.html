About

a little bit about me..

My name is Aliyah-Katherine Reynolds, and I’m a 17-year-old artist currently attending Utah Military Academy. Ever since I was young, I’ve had a deep passion for art, spending countless hours sketching, painting, and experimenting with different styles and techniques. Over the years, my love for creativity has only grown stronger, pushing me to refine my skills and explore new artistic possibilities. Art is more than just a hobby for me—it’s a way to express emotions, tell stories, and connect with others on a deeper level. Whether it’s digital illustrations, traditional paintings, or character designs, I strive to create pieces that capture personality and meaning. Through this website, I hope to not only showcase my work but also build connections with those who appreciate art as much as I do. If you’re interested in commissioning a piece, I’d love to bring your ideas to life! Feel free to reach out, and let’s create something amazing together.

Recent Artworks!

Japanese Town Corner

This artwork was created using Prismacolor pencils as a study in three-point perspective. I am especially proud of the night scene, where the rich, vibrant colors create a striking and immersive atmosphere. The contrast between light and shadow enhances the depth and energy of the composition. However, while I aimed for a seamless blend of colors in the daylight portion, the palette did not harmonize as effectively as I had envisioned. Despite this, the piece reflects my growth in perspective and color theory, making it a valuable step in my artistic journey.

Moss

This artwork combines technology and nature in a surreal way, featuring glass light bulbs filled with strange, otherworldly scenes. Suspended by aged metal fixtures, the bulbs contain skeletal remains, twisting creatures, and plant-like forms, symbolizing decay, transformation, and renewal. The deep green background resembles overgrown foliage or an eerie void, emphasizing the contrast between artificial and natural elements. A distressed wooden frame, decorated with moss, dried plants, and broken wood, adds to the artwork’s raw and immersive feel. Using watercolor or ink along with three-dimensional details, the piece creates a unique blend of illustration and installation art.

The Last Of Us

This artwork is a fan piece inspired by one of my favorite games. I painted a Clicker-stage zombie, focusing on its eerie and grotesque details to capture the unsettling nature of its design. I wanted to emphasize the textures and organic growths that define this stage of infection, bringing out the horror elements that make Clickers so iconic. Through careful attention to color and shading, I aimed to create a striking and immersive portrayal of this terrifying creature.
